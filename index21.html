The Guest

Year: 2014
Badass:
Dark:

A stranger arrives at a peaceful suburban home claiming a link to the family's deceased son--but this situation quickly takes a turn to the extreme. A wild ride of a film with a great soundtrack.

Austin Powers: the Spy Who Shagged Me

Year: 1999
Mike Myers:
Nuts:

Austin Powers is back on the case when Doctor Evil travels through time to steal his mojo. This film introduced many of the most memorable characters in the series and is still pretty damn hilarious.

The Tree of Life

Year: 2011
Art Film:
Surreal:

An undeniable epic of a film, The Tree of Life is truly a portrait of life itself. Breathtaking visuals and heavy themes. Extremely dry.

Observe and Report

Year: 2009
Fucked Up:
Bizarre:

Seth Rogen plays a mentally ill security guard who dreams of being a police officer. But along the way, he goes through some pretty hardcore shit and finds his true calling.

Zootopia

Year: 2016
Disney:
Fun:

A strong recent entry into the Disney catalog, Zootopia is a lively buddy-cop detective thriller. Great voice acting and animation.

A Scanner Darkly

Adaptation:
Mindfuck:

Keanu Reeves stars as a half-animated detective in this modern adaptation of a Philip Dick novel. I expect lots of drugs, madness and paranoia.

The Godfather

One of the Best:
Acting:

It's hard to find a more iconic film than The Godfather. An epic and unforgettable mafia drama, widely recognized as a full-blown masterpiece.

Walk Hard: The Dewey Cox Story

Judd Apatow:
John C. Reilly:

This film chronicles the life of fictional musician Dewey Cox as he grapples with the stress of fame. Cameos and jokes abound in this surprisingly thoughtful parody.

The Ballad of Buster Scruggs

Stylish:
Coen Bros.:

I know almost nothing about this movie. Based on what I've seen, my suspicion is that this one is bizarre (and possibly musical).

Rat Race

Great Set Pieces:
Good Movie:

There's some great shit in this weird ensemble B-movie that's a remake of some old movie no one's ever seen. I recommend getting wasted before you watch.

Who Framed Roger Rabbit

Technical Marvel:
Great Film:

Bob Hoskins kicks ass in this one-of-a-kind detective drama. Mixes comedy and tragedy, noir and cartoon, Disney and Looney Tunes (et al).

Darkman

Sam Raimi:
Wacky:

The only super hero film that I actually love, Darkman is like the first Spider-Man on acid. Liam Neeson plays a brilliant scientist who gets horribly attacked by the mob, and then uses his genius to set things right.

The Producers

Mel Brooks:
Clever:

Two movie "producers" realize that a terrible film can make more money than a good one. The result is a wacky, fast-paced stunner of comedy gold.

Nightbreed

Clive Barker:
Demented:

I have no idea how to describe this film. It's about a monster man who starts a monster war, and it's one of the most bizarre and chaotic movies I've ever seen.

Sleeper

Iconic:
One of the Best:

One of Woody Allen's most memorable comedies. A nebbish New Yorker gets frozen and re-thawed in the distant future--and then inadvertently stumbles into a wild adventure.

Grave of the Fireflies

Well-Reviewed:
Quality Anime:

Two kids in Japan get firebombed out of their home during WWII. I'm told it's very sad. Though I've never seen it, I've heard it mentioned numerous times and always fondly.

Beavis and Butthead Do America

Assinine:
Hilarious:

How do you extend the relatively simple format of Beavis and Butthead into a feature film? Mike Judge found a way. This movie also counts as my tribute to Bruce Willis, who retired from acting this year. God bless, Bruce.

Once Upon a Time... in Hollywood

Tarantino:
Beautiful:

WARNING: make sure you know a little about Charles Manson going in. Yet another late-career masterpiece from Quentin Tarantino. This incredible piece takes its time and sets up one of my favorite endings in any movie. A must-watch.

Terminator 2: Judgement Day

Action:
Sci-Fi:

Now the Terminator has to fight a second, even more badass terminator. Often condsidered the archetypal action/sci-fi film.

Parasite

Fucked Up:
Masterpiece:

A lower-class korean family cons their way into the home of one much richer. Easily one of the best films of the last decade, and one I genuinely love.

Mary Poppins

Classic:
Disney Magic:

One of the older Disney classics, I think this one's about a magical nanny who comes to hang out with some british kids. Haven't seen it in probably twenty five years.

World's Greatest Dad

Dark Comedy:
Touching:

Robin Williams is a father who loses his son through an embarassing accident--but he soon discovers a bizarre way to keep his son's legacy alive. This dark indie comedy is insane but surprisingly touching.

Blue Ruin

Sad:
Fucked Up:

A man whose parents are murdered can't move past the trauma. But when the killer is released years later, our reluctant anti-hero decides to exact revenge. A fascinating and melancholy indie character study.

Muppet Treasure Island

Awesome Adaptation:
Musical:

The classic tale of Treasure Island, re-imagined for a modern audience. Get ready to watch Miss Piggy compete with Tim Curry for the most over-the-top performance.
