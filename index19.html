Synecdoche, New York

Phillip Seymour Hoffman:
Depressing:

A nightmarish, abstract art film about life and (mostly) death. I think it's about a playwright who's chasing his life's masterpiece. Extremely rough watch.

Osmosis Jones

Action:
Comedy:

This animated goofball buddy-cop film takes place in the body of Bill Murray, with all the characters being microbes and stuff. It's actually pretty good. Creative gimmicks, good chemistry, Bill Murray.

Dead Man

Slow:
Beautiful:

Dark and bizarre, this Jarmusch film is about a hapless guy who goes on a brutal journey through the American wild west. Features lots of haunting imagery.

One Hour Photo

Dark:
Sinister:

A dark and wild ride featuring Robin Williams as like a creepy stalker guy. If you want to experience commercial film development and other fucked up shit, this is a great choice.

Secret of NIMH

Family Film:
Pretty Great:

A more serious kid's film, this Don Bluth epic stars a widow field mouse who's trying to save her sick children. This one's characterized by a darker tone and lush animation.

Waking Life

Unique:
Philosophical:

This truly weird anthology film is just all over the map. We follow a protagonist as he travels from dream to dream after having died(?). Has some really cool indie-type art stuff.

Half Baked

Quirky:
Dark:

Dave Chappelle has to save one of his buddies by selling medical-grade marijuana he steals from a lab. But this plan soons runs into some big complications.

Rifftrax: Super Mario Bros.

Comedy:
Wild Ride:

The cast of MST3K riffs on this video game classic. A great two-for-one, because Super Mario Bros. is worth a watch in its own right.

Blazing Saddles

Hilarious:
From a Different Era:

An absolutely classic Mel Brooks film. Basically it's an indictment of race relations in America that devolves into a parody fever dream.

The Postman

Post-Apocalyptic:
Kevin Costner:

In the post-apocalyptic world, one man finds a postman's outfit and starts delivering letters. This causes a war basically. Three hours long.

Kung Pow: Enter the Fist

Absurd:
Quotable:

Some guy took a bunch of old kung-fu movie footage and cut it together, added a dub track, and made his own movie. It came out really weird.

Apt Pupil

Dark:
Uncomfortable:

Grisly-as-fuck holocaust thriller. Probably the most dismal film this year, despite that being a very competitive category.

Little Miss Sunshine

Modern Classic:
Memorable:

Love this one. A little girl and her offbeat family must travel cross-country to enter her into a child pageant. But can their relationships survive the stress?

Thank You for Smoking

Entertaining:
Philosophical:

Sharp and snappy parody about a tobacco lobbyist and his journey through politics. Great acting and awesome dialogue.

Spotlight

Gritty:
Biographical:

This somber journalist drama is about the discovery and investigation of systemic diddling in the Catholic church. Not as dark as it could be, mostly just serious.

Moonrise Kingdom

Wes Anderson:
Good Shit:

This film is about young love, camping, and lots of other stuff. Highly stylized and featuring much of the Wes Anderson crew.

Brazil

Spectacular:
Depressing:

Jesus Christ. It's about a 1984-style dystopia and one poor dumb bastard who gets wrapped up in the system. Miserable but shockingly beautiful at times.

Cool World

Weird:
Forgotten:

A very strange little piece that's kind of a knock-off of Who Framed Roger Rabbit? Brad Pitt is so super young in this movie and it gets really weird.

Yellow Submarine

Beatles:
Classic:

A kickass Beatles cartoon. It has adventures, wry british comedy, and SO much Beatles music put to visuals. One hell of a ride.

The Departed

Drama:
Dark:

This wild crime drama epic follows two cops, both entangled in the dangerous world of the Irish mob. Extremely elaborate and masterfully wrought.

Lars and the Real Girl

Quirky:
Gosling:

A quiet and melancholy little slice-of-life drama. Lars is a sad and lonely man who has a psychotic break, and his little town rallies together to help him. Very sweet.

Tommy

Insane:
Musical:

This rock opera is completely batshit insane. It's about a little Hellen Keller boy, and also about post-war London and God. Amazing musical scenes.

There Will Be Blood

Dark:
Daniel Day Lewis:

Hardcore drama. An evil man's obsessive journey to build an oil empire. So much stuff happens, it's great.

Dogma

Comedy:
Unique:

A very strange comedy/adventure film about two fallen angels seeking to get back into heaven. Jay and Silent Bob are there.
